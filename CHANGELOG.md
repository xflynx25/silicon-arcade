# Changelog

## 2026-07-16

- **CADENCE**: added a twelfth game, and the first that rewards **temporal precision** instead of spatial twitch — hitting on the beat, not aim or reflex. The new engine primitive is `clock.ts`: a WebAudio beat clock anchored on `audioCtx.currentTime`, with a lookahead scheduler (a plain `setInterval` ticker that wakes every ~25ms and schedules every 16th-note step whose audio time falls within the next ~100ms directly onto `osc.start(when)`) and a beat/bar model (`beatOfTime`/`timeOfBeat`, re-anchored on BPM change so a tempo tune re-times the whole game for free). `requestAnimationFrame` only draws; it never judges a hit. Hit judging is a simple diff against ±35ms (perfect) / ±75ms (good) windows, honest enough to build the whole game on. `calibrate.ts` adds a first-run latency calibration — tap along to a bare metronome for 8 beats, outliers trimmed, the averaged offset persisted to `localStorage` and subtracted from every judgement thereafter; reachable (and repeatable) from the title via `C`. `music.ts` is a scale/chord engine (C minor pentatonic, so every on-beat hit is a consonant note — you can only ever be wrong about *when*, never *what*) plus a layered generative synth (kick/hat always on; bass → arp → pad unlock as accuracy sustains, and drop back out on a miss), scheduled onto the same clock via `clock.subscribe`. Movement keys become lane keys (P1 `A S D W`, P2 arrows, `Shift` accent) — deliberately fewer inputs than the action games, and lanes capped at 4/player against keyboard-rollover on cheap boards. Two modes: **Trade** (co-op call-and-response — the game lights a short phrase on the acting player's lanes, they perform it, the other player echoes it on the next bar; clean handoffs raise a shared groove meter and lengthen the phrase, a bad handoff costs groove and bottoming out ends the run; score = longest groove sustained in bars) and **Clash** (competitive — notes stream to both lanes over a fixed 90s track, each player scoring their own accuracy; a hit streak charges Syncopation, which briefly shifts the rival's incoming notes off-grid, a fair attack on their timing that never touches their controls; higher accuracy score when the track ends wins). Reclaims the rhythm genre ECHO abandoned when it rewrote into stealth-survival (see 2026-07-03) with a clock built for it this time. Leaderboards wired for both boards (`cadence`/`trade`, `cadence`/`clash`). Wired `dev:cadence`, the workspace, `assemble-dist.mjs`, the arcade launcher tile/leaderboard tab, and README.

## 2026-07-15

- **RELAY**: added an eleventh game, and the first **asymmetric** one — one shared keyboard split into two complementary, unequal roles instead of a mirrored P1/P2. **Pilot** (P1) drives an avatar but only sees a small fog bubble around it; **Navigator** (P2) sees the whole map but has no movement control, only a cursor and a capped set of pings (go-here waypoint / danger arrow / wait — only two live at once, so the budget forces them to talk instead of just pointing). The one new render technique is `fog.ts`: a single cached radial-gradient alpha mask, rebuilt only when the fog radius knob changes, composited with `destination-in` onto a bright "Pilot vision" scratch layer that's blitted over an always-visible dim "Navigator" layer — no per-frame `shadowBlur`, matching the perf lessons already in this file. Two modes: **Escort** (descend a procedurally-generated vertical maze of moving mines and timed gates to the exit before the clock runs out; reaching a waypoint ping "locks" it with a chime and a small time bonus; score = depth reached) and **Defuse** (a symbol appears on the Pilot's panel, only the Navigator's manual says what input sequence it means, and the manual never reveals which entry is active, so the two of you have to describe and search; three wrong/late entries ends the run; score = panels defused). A shared rising-tension audio pad (one persistent oscillator ramped by nearest-mine distance, not retriggered per frame) is the one info channel both roles get regardless of role. Leaderboards wired for both boards (`relay`/`escort`, `relay`/`defuse`). Wired `dev:relay`, the workspace, `assemble-dist.mjs`, the arcade launcher tile/leaderboard tab, and README.

- **GHOST**: added a tenth game, and the first to touch **time**. Every 12s lap is silently recorded into a per-tick ring buffer (`recorder.ts`, the one new engine primitive), then replayed as a translucent ghost of your past self — pure playback off the shared fixed-timestep loop, so it can never desync from what you actually did (verified with a dev-only self-test that steps a Ghost through a frozen Recording and asserts it reproduces the buffer exactly). Three modes: **Chase** (co-op relay — carry a shared orb to the goal, ghosts still holding Grab keep carrying/blocking), **Haunt** (competitive — collect sparks, but touching any ghost, yours or your rival's, drops your streak and stuns you), and **Duel** (competitive strike/parry combat — ghosts re-strike on the exact ticks their originals did, with a telegraph flash before each replayed strike; best of 5, no leaderboard). Live **Echo Depth** knob (`[`/`]`, 1–5 ghosts per player). Leaderboards wired for Chase (legs completed) and Haunt (winning score). Wired `dev:ghost`, the workspace, `assemble-dist.mjs`, and the arcade launcher tile/leaderboard tab.

- **LEADERBOARDS**: expanded beyond TETHER to five more game modes. Extracted the shared client into `@arcade/leaderboard` (`shared/`) so games import one workspace package instead of copying `leaderboard.ts`. Wired **CIPHER** (rounds completed), **NOVA** Flares (survival time) and Rings (60s score), **RICOCHET** Rally (rally count — pauses auto-restart for initials/board), and **ECHO** Core/Grid (waves survived). Each game gets the dev leaderboard plugin on its Vite config. The arcade launcher Leaderboards overlay now lists all six games with per-board tabs.

- **SALVO**: fixed shells vanishing mid-flight even on the Infinite ricochet preset — a hidden `shellLife` fuse (4–9s) was killing shells regardless of bounce count. Presets now expire shells only on bounce cap or tank hit. Also fixed obstacle slides counting a bounce every frame when a shell grazed a wall edge.
- **SALVO**: added ammunition controls on the title screen — concurrent shells (`4`), fire rate (`5`), and per-round ammo pool (`6`, one press = one shot even with triple-shot). **Variety** mode (`7`, default) re-rolls unlocked settings each round; `Shift+digit` locks individual rows so you can e.g. keep powerups on while maps and ricochet rules shuffle. HUD shows the active round config and remaining ammo when the pool is finite.

## 2026-07-06

- **LEADERBOARDS**: added old-arcade high-score leaderboards with no database. Scores persist as **one JSON blob per game+board in Vercel Blob** (no Neon/Postgres), served through two tiny zero-config serverless functions at `/api/leaderboard` (`GET` to list top 20, `POST` to submit `{ game, board, name, score }`). No auth — just type your initials (A–Z / 0–9, arcade-style) when you make the board. A Vite dev plugin (`scripts/leaderboard-dev-plugin.ts`) serves the identical contract from a local `.data/` file so `pnpm dev` works with no cloud. Shared pure logic lives in `api/_leaderboard-core.ts` (validation, sort, trim) so dev and prod behave the same. **TETHER** is wired first: metric is seconds survived, one board per difficulty (easy/normal/hard); the Run Complete overlay now shows the board and prompts for initials on a qualifying run. Other games opt in by importing the same client helper. Optional `LEADERBOARD_TOKEN` shared secret guards writes. **Fully optional and self-disabling:** with no Blob store linked, the API reports the leaderboard as disabled and the games show no leaderboard UI at all (no board, no prompt, no errors) — a fresh clone/deploy Just Works; link a Blob store only when you want it (see README).

## 2026-07-05

- **NOVA**: sharpened the three mode identities so each mode has a distinct verb — **Fight**, **Survive**, **Collect**. **Duel** now awards rounds on **Flare-strike** hits (not just ram/boundary), lowered ram kill thresholds (280+ speed, 1.15× ratio), perfect Shield parries **stagger** the attacker, and the playable band is slightly wider. **Flares** adds corona creep each wave, rotating bolt patterns (burst / spiral / crossfire), telegraphed aimed shots, slow homing bolts from wave 3, and **three shared lives** with respawn. **Rings** adds combo chains (3s window, wall bounce resets), linked gold pairs (+6 when both comets collect within 4s), risk rings near the corona (5 pts), and Shield **magnet pulse** on nearby rings. Extracted mode copy to `nova/src/modes.ts` with per-mode help text; updated title menu, HUD (lives, combo), and README.
- **ECHO**: anti-camp gameplay update — camping both players on the Core and spamming was the dominant strategy, so the whole game now punishes it. Added a title-screen mode picker (`1`/`2`, à la ricochet): **Core** is the classic single central base, **Grid** scatters three nodes in a triangle and foes swarm the nearest — you can't hold them from one spot, and losing even one node ends the run. Reworked **resonance** to reward spreading out: pings closer than a minimum separation no longer connect, and blast radius/damage scale with how far apart the two origins are (plus a short global cooldown so it can't chain every frame). Added three enemy types: **sirens** park at range and drain a base from afar (self-revealed while channeling — you must leave the base and hunt them), **broods** burst into a spray of darters when killed (so point-blank kills at a base backfire), and heavy **brutes** that shrug off single strikes and reward sustained fire/resonance. Standing on a hurt base slowly **repairs** it, personal light shrinks as waves escalate (late waves lean on pings), and the HUD/overlay/render are mode-aware with per-base health rings, threat warnings, and siren drain beams. Generalized the single `coreHealth` scalar into a `bases` array throughout.

## 2026-07-03 (continued)

- **POLARITY**: fixed the playing field rendering in a tiny top-left corner on HiDPI/Retina displays — screen-shake used `setTransform`, which wiped out the device-pixel-ratio scale applied at startup.
- **POLARITY**: toned down dash/burst VFX to stop frame stalls — removed per-particle and entity `shadowBlur`, capped the particle pool, replaced the expanding dash grab ring with a static fade ring, and simplified shockwave/trail draws (same pattern as vortex/tether).
- **ECHO**: removed `shadowBlur` from expanding ping rings, enemies, core, and players; capped particles and switched to cheap dual-disc glows.
- **RICOCHET**: capped the particle pool (320) and fixed `life`/`maxLife` drift so burst moments cannot balloon the pool.
- **ECHO**: full redesign from a one-axis rhythm game into **Blackout**, a co-op survival game driven by echolocation. Two players defend a shared **Core** at the center of a dark arena through 6 escalating waves of husks (drifters, fast darters, tanky husks) that crawl in from the black and are invisible until revealed. **Ping** (primary) fires a sonar ring that lights up and knocks back every husk it sweeps; **Strike** (secondary) destroys husks in melee range — you position by memory of what your pings revealed. When both players' pings overlap they **resonate**, flashing the whole arena and blasting everything nearby (carrying over Echo's old "both players lock together" identity). Husks that reach the Core bite its health; drop it to zero and the dark wins. Removed the entire BPM/beat/tempo system (audio beat scheduler, `onBeat`, tempo controls) and rewrote the game, input, audio, and main wiring around the new loop.
- **NOVA**: added a sixth game — an orbital slingshot duel. A central star exerts real gravity on two comets; players thrust to shape their orbit, dive close for a gravity-assist speed boost, and ram their rival — the faster comet shatters the slower one, near-matched speeds just bounce. Reuses the vortex feel: hold `Shift`/`RShift` to charge a **Flare** lunge, `Space`/`Enter` for a timed **Shield** parry that reflects a ram. Two death lines — burn up in the star's corona or drift past the void edge — plus a live `[`/`]` **Gravity** knob (0.4×–2.5×). Title-screen picker (`1`/`2`/`3`): **Duel** (competitive best of 5), **Flares** (co-op survival vs plasma bolts, lethal corona/void, best time), **Rings** (co-op ring collection on a 60s clock, soft bounce walls). Wired `dev:nova` and the workspace, updated README.
